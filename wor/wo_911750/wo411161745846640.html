1.1 General Organization

1.1.1 Overview

  1. WCS/TMA subsystem is integrated within the Combat Information Control System (CICS) developed by Indian Navy and functionally interfaced to the Combat Information Subsystem (CIS). It is composed of two functional modules:
    1. A Target Motion Analysis (TMA) software module
    2. A Weapon Control Systems (WCS) software module.
  2. TMA is able to process tracks coming from the Sensors (System Tracks) via the
    CICSCommand Information Control System
    middleware. The computation of trajectory for these tracks uses bearings and, if available, frequencies and/or ranges history. It aims at the adoption of a solution (Track's Tactical Parameters) by the tactical operator.
  3. WCS provides the capability to engage torpedoes, attack surface targets and sub surface targets. Targets parameters are provided to
    WCSWeapon Control Subsystem
    by CIS. The launching sequence is fully managed by WCS.
  4. The sub-system operation is performed in the
    CICCommand Information Centre
    by means of five (5) Dual screens Multi Function Consoles (DMFC), where up to four (4) are available for WCS/TMA purpose.
    WCSWeapon Control Subsystem
    can also be operated in the torpedo room by means of one (1) Local Launching Console (LLC).
  5. In terms of weapons launching capabilities, the sub-system is designed to launch and wireguide both,
    T2Type 2
    and
    T1Type 1
    torpedoes.
  6. The weapons themselves and their physical integration within the tubes are not part of the Sub: system delivery.
  7. The Sub-system will have an interface mainly with:
    1. The Combat Information Subsystem (The Navigation Complex, sensors tracks acquisition and management)
    2. The weapon complex including the Torpedo Tube Preparation System (TTPS) and torpedoes.
